NASA Kennedy Space Center: Spacecraft Pressure Pane Structural Health Monitoring

Celestine is working with the NASA Kennedy Space Center Analytical Laboratories and Applied Physics Laboratory to support window pane stress and structural health monitoring for the ISS and future spacecraft. Her research involves collecting stress-induced birefringence measurements from Shuttle-era window panes with micrometeoroid impacts. She is writing an algorithm that linearizes images of the defect and applies a mathematical model utilizing birefringence stress analysis to predict the breaking point of the window panes. This research supports the development of a prototype device for astronauts to use while in space to determine the structural health of window panels.

NASA Kennedy Space Center: Liquid Propellant Tanks Structural Health Monitoring

Celestine was selected to work with the NASA Kennedy Space Center Advanced Engineering Development Branch to to commercialize a technology she supported and developed during her research experiences at college. During this internship she characterized the structural health and modal signature of a composite-overwrap liquid propellant tank. She accomplished this through writing and implementing algorithms that apply broadband white noise signals to piezoelectric actuators, collect data from piezoelectric sensors, and process this data using Fourier analysis to produce frequency response functions, which she used to determine resonant modes of the tank structure. In regards to structrual health monitoring, high frequency modes will resonate unexpectedly when a composite layer of the tank has been damaged or is defective. Her results agree with finite element models and is in the process of being published.
